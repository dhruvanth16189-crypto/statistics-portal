Probability & Probability Distributions

4.6 Continuous Distribution: Normal Distribution

The Normal curve represents a perfectly symmetrical distribution. A key concept is the Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7):

  • 68% of data falls within 1 standard deviation (μ ± σ).
  • 95% of data falls within 2 standard deviations (μ ± 2σ).
  • 99.7% of data falls within 3 standard deviations (μ ± 3σ).

4.7 Odds Ratio & 4.8 Poisson Distribution

Odds Ratio (OR): Calculated as (a×d)/(b×c). An OR > 1 means exposure increases risk.

Poisson Distribution: Used for rare events/counts. The mean equals the variance (λ). Excel: POISSON.DIST.

Correlation & Regression

5.1 to 5.4 Meaning & Analysis

Correlation (r): Ranges from -1 to 1. Measures the strength and direction of a linear association. Found via CORREL in Excel.

Regression: Models relationship to predict dependent variable (response) based on independent variable (predictor).

Equation: Y = a + bX
(a = Intercept, b = Slope)

Maths For Data Science

6.1 Outliers & Boxplots

IQR: Q3 - Q1. Lower whisker = Q1 - 1.5×IQR. Boxplots identify these outliers visually.

6.3 to 6.7 Missing Value Treatment

  • Listwise: Deletes entire row if any data is missing.
  • Pairwise: Uses maximum available data.
  • Imputation: Mean (bad for skewed data), Median (best for skewed), Mode (categorical).
  • KNN (K-Nearest Neighbors): Uses similar records to keep relationships intact.
